Principal Investigator

Dr. Shashi Kiran
Assistant Professor · Department of Biochemistry · University of Hyderabad

Dr. Shashi Kiran completed his Ph.D. in Biochemistry at the Centre for DNA Fingerprinting and Diagnostics (CDFD), Hyderabad, and subsequently trained as a Research Associate at the University of Virginia (UVa, 2014–2020), where his work uncovered how the HPV oncoprotein hijacks the USP46 deubiquitinase to reprogram protein stability and drive cervical and head-and-neck cancers. He established his independent lab at the University of Hyderabad in 2023 as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Biochemistry.

The lab's primary research focus is on identifying and therapeutically exploiting vulnerabilities within the ubiquitination pathway in cancers. The approach begins with a mechanistic dissection of how specific E3 ubiquitin ligases and deubiquitinases regulate the stability of proteins that cancer cells depend on for survival and growth. By mapping these dependencies, the lab pinpoints druggable nodes in the ubiquitin network. Current projects investigate the USP46 deubiquitinase in HPV-driven cancers, the CRL4-Cdt2 ubiquitin ligase complex as a regulator of cancer cell proliferation in breast cancer, and the role of extrachromosomal DNA amplifications in fuelling oncogenesis and drug resistance in cervical cancer. Once a target is validated, the lab employs drug design and in vitro screening pipelines to identify and optimise specific small-molecule inhibitors and peptide-based tools, working towards mechanism-based cancer therapeutics.

The second focus of the lab is on advancing CRISPR-Cas9-based genome editing, with a particular emphasis on achieving efficient biallelic knock-in edits in diploid cells. A key challenge in studying endogenous proteins is the inability to precisely modify both chromosomal copies simultaneously — the lab is developing strategies to overcome this using the CD101 gene as a model system. Beyond basic biology, this biallelic editing platform is being applied to generate accurate cellular and animal models of genetic diseases, and to explore CRISPR-based approaches for the therapeutic correction of pathogenic mutations.

His research is supported by grants from ANRF, ICMR, DST-SERB, and UGC, and has been published across leading journals in cell biology, cancer research, and genomics. He is deeply committed to mentoring the next generation of researchers in a rigorous, inclusive, and collaborative environment.
